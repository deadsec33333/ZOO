Meet Churro
Mandrillus sphinx

Born in early November 2024, Churro is the newest addition to the Arizona Zoos primate family. As a howler monkey, hes part of one of the loudest monkey species on Earth, even though his tiny size doesnt give that away just yet. Named "Churro" for his warm, cinnamon-brown fur that resembles the sweet treat, this little howler has already begun to charm zoo visitors with his expressive eyes and tiny squeaks. In a few months, as he grows, hell start developing the signature deep growl of his species, but for now, hes content to cling to his mom, nestled in her soft fur as they move through the canopy.

Born to a Family of Singers

Howler monkeys are known for their impressive vocal abilities. Even though Churro is currently quiet, hes destined to become a part of the daily chorus that can be heard echoing across the zoo. Adult howlers use their deep, resonating calls to communicate across vast distancesoften up to three miles in dense forests. These calls are a way to establish territory and keep in touch with family members. In the wild, they live in groups of about 10 to 15, but here at the zoo, Churro is growing up with a close-knit family of five. By the time hes older, hell be joining in the vocalizations that have made his species famous.

A Taste for the Treetops

Like all howler monkeys, Churro is built for life in the trees. Even at a young age, he has a strong, prehensile tail that he can use almost like a fifth limb. It helps him grip branches as he explores his new surroundings. For now, hes sticking close to his mother, but soon hell be bounding across branches and learning to navigate the leafy treetops. In the wild, howler monkeys spend most of their time high in the canopy, munching on leaves, fruits, and flowers. Here at the zoo, Churro is slowly being introduced to new foods, from soft leaves to small pieces of fruit, and it wont be long before he develops a taste for his favorite snacks.

Diet: seeds, fruit, roots, bark, insects, small vertebrates, grasses

Zoo Diet: formulated feed, starch, veggies, fruits, greens, browse

Habitat: tropical forests

Weight: 25 – 70 lbs
